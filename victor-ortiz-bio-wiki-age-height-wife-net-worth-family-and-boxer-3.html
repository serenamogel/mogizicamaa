Victor Ortiz Biography

Victor Ortiz is an American professional boxer and film actor who was born in Garden City, Kansas, the U.S. He held the WBC welterweight title in the year 2011 and was formerly rated as one of the world’s top three active welterweights by most sporting news as well as boxing websites, such as The Ring magazine, BoxRec, and ESPN.

His crowd-pleasing and aggressive fighting style also made him the year 2008 ESPN Prospect of the Year. Besides boxing, Ortiz has had roles in the films The Expendables 3 in 2014 and Southpaw in 2015 and made a cameo appearance in the TV series Ray Donovan.

Victor Ortiz Family

Ortiz was born and raised by his parents in Garden City, Kansas. He is the third born of four children of Mexican parents. At the age of 7, Ortiz’s mother abandoned her family. Shortly thereafter, Ortiz started boxing at the insistence of his father named Victor Ortiz Sr, an alcoholic who often beat his children following his wife’s departure.

In an interview, Victor said, “I hated that lady. I drew her a card once with a little rose on it and I gave it to her. She just threw it down and said ‘What do I want that shit for?’ That’s when I picked up boxing. Then my Dad started screwing up, drinking.”

Ortiz’s father also abandoned the family 5 years after their mother left, which forced Ortiz and his three siblings into the Kansas foster care system. Ortiz was 12 years old at the time. His older sister became a legal adult in the year 2002 and moved to Denver, Colorado. Ortiz alongside his younger brother left Kansas and moved in with her.

Victor Ortiz vs Mayweather

Floyd Mayweather Jr. reported on 7 June 2011, via Twitter that he and Ortiz had an agreement to fight on 17 September 2011. The fight was for Ortiz’s WBC Welterweight belt.

The fight took place at MGM Grand Garden Arena. From round one, Mayweather tagged Ortiz repeatedly. Mayweather seemed in control through the first three rounds, judges’ scores 30–27, 30–27, and 29–28 for Mayweather, in the fourth round, Ortiz found success, landing a few shots as well as stinging Mayweather prior to bulling him into the corner.

Out of frustration, Ortiz rammed Mayweather in the face with an intentional headbutt. The referee, Joe Cortez, called a timeout and docked Ortiz a point for the foul. As the fight resumed, Ortiz approached Mayweather in the center of the ring and tried to apologize, and even hugged Mayweather.

After apologizing, Ortiz had his hands down, and Mayweather unloaded a left hook as well as a flush right hand to Ortiz’s chin. Ortiz dropped and was unable to beat Cortez’s count as the crowd of 14,687 were furious at the ruined fight.

“In the ring, you have to protect yourself at all times,” Mayweather said. “After it happened, we touched gloves, and we were back to fighting, and then I threw the left hook and right hand after the break. You just gotta protect yourself at all times.” Mayweather also said he would give Ortiz a rematch, which never came to fruition.
