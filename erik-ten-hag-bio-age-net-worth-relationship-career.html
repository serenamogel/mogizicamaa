Former Dutch professional football player and current manager Erik ten Hag. He has represented FC Utrecht, FC Twente, De Graafschap, and RKC Waalwijk as a center defender. In 2002, he ceased his active playing career at the age of 32. He has not made any international appearances on behalf of his country.

2012 saw the appointment of him as manager of the Go Ahead Eagles of the Eerste Divisie by club shareholder Marc Overmars. From 2013 until 2015, Heiko Vogel assumed the role of coach for Bayern Munich II in his place.

Subsequently, in the summer of 2015, he was appointed sporting director and head coach of FC Utrecht, positions he held until 2017.

His teams have advanced to the 2019 UEFA Champions League semifinals and won two Eredivisie titles, one KNVB Cup, and one Johan Cruyff Shield while he was the head coach of Ajax.

In Haaksbergen, he began his football career with the amateur club SV Bon Boys. On December 13, 1989, he debuted professionally for FC Twente in the 66th minute against FC Groningen as a substitute for Robin Schmidt in a 5-0 draw.

He appeared in fourteen matches during his debut season. Following that, he joined De Graafschap in the First Division for the 1990-91 season, where he led the club to the Eerste Divisie championship ten years before his cup victory with Twente. Although he returned to De Graafschap for the 1991-92 season, he appeared in only half of the matches.

Despite having appeared in a significant number of games for FC Twente throughout two seasons, beginning in the summer of 1992, he transferred to RKC for the 1994-95 season to join FC Utrecht the following year.

Throughout two seasons, he appeared in nearly every match for both organizations. In the 1996–97 season, he made a permanent return to Twente. He won the KNVB Cup with Twente during the 2000-2001 season.

He retired from active participation in 2002, while still playing for Twente, at the age of 32, after the conclusion of the 2001-02 Eredivisie season.

2012 saw the appointment of him as manager of the Go Ahead Eagles of the Eerste Divisie by club shareholder Marc Overmars. On May 26, 2013, after seventeen years, he assisted Go Ahead Eagles in their promotion to the Eredivisie during his rookie campaign as coach.

The following day, Foeke Booy succeeded him as the head coach of the team, following his protracted resignation. From 2013 until 2015, Heiko Vogel assumed the role of coach for Bayern Munich II in his place. On May 22, 2015, his final match as head coach of Bayern Munich II ended in a 1-0 defeat to Nurnberg II.

Following his appointment as Sporting Director and head coach of FC Utrecht in the summer of 2015, he guided the club to fifth place in its inaugural season.

Additionally, the club advanced to the KNVB Cup final before succumbing 2-1 to Feyenoord. He received the Rinus Michels Award, the Eredivisie’s highest distinction, after the season.

FC Utrecht qualified for the Eredivisie play-offs during the 2016-17 season, thereby securing the possibility of competing in European football the following season.

He claimed his first management championship with Ajax on 5 May 2019, defeating Willem II in the final of the 2018-19 KNVB Cup. Furthermore, on May 15, 2019, he led the club to the league championship with 86 points, surpassing PSV by three points.

Two days later, he was honored with the Rinus Michels Award as the preeminent coach of the Eredivisie. He prolonged his contract with Ajax through 2022 on June 20, 2019.
