It is super easy to make and only requires a few ingredients that you probably already have in your pantry.  As always, you can find the full printable recipe with complete instructions in the recipe card at the end of this post. Let’s take a look at what you’ll need:

    • cream cheese – you’ll want to make sure your cream cheese is fully softened so it combines with the other ingredients easily. I do recommend full fat cream cheese.
    • sour cream – or plain greek yogurt or even mayonnaise will work in this recipe. It’s an optional ingredient but does add some nice flavor and makes for a softer, smoother dip.
    • pickle juice – straight from the pickle jar!
    • dill pickles – I love dill pickles but sweet pickles can be used as well. Dice them nice and finely for this pickle dip recipe.
    • sweet relish – the relish blends with the cream cheese and sour cream to nicely distribute the pickle flavor.
    • dried dill – emphasizes the dill flavor from the pickles and rounds out the flavor.
    • garlic – I use one clove but if you’re a garlic lover, you can use more.
    • salt and black pepper – for seasoning.

    Serve this Dill Pickle Dip recipe with crackers, pretzels, chips or vegetables. I love to provide an assortment of dippers for this tasty dip.

    How To Make Dill Pickle Dip

  • Beat the cream cheese until you have a smooth and creamy texture.
  • Mix in the sour cream, if using, until fully combined.
  • Add in the pickle juice, diced dill pickle, sweet relish, dried dill, garlic, salt and pepper.
  • Mix until everything is well combined.
  • Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.
  • Sit out at room temperature to soften the dip if needed.
  • Serve with your favorite crackers, chips or veggies!

    • Storage Information

    Store any leftover dip in an airtight container in the fridge. It will last for about 2 to 3 days. I don’t recommend freezing this dip because of the dairy.

    Pickle Dip FAQs

    Can I make this ahead of time?

    Yes, you can make this dill pickle dip recipe ahead of time. Just mix all the ingredients and store them in an airtight container in the fridge. It will last for about 2-3 days.

    Can I freeze this dip?

    I don’t recommend it. Freezing dairy can be tricky and the texture will not be ideal.

    What should I serve with dill pickle dip?

    This easy dip recipe is great with crackers, chips, pretzels and veggies. You can also use it as a spread for sandwiches or wraps!

    Can I make this dip vegan?

    Sure! Swap in vegan cream cheese for a tasty vegan dip.

    Trish’s Tips and Tricks 

    This recipe is delicious and easy but check out my favorite tips and tricks for more information for the best results:

    • The cream cheese should be softened so that it’s easy to mix. You can leave it out on the counter for a bit or microwave it for 20 to 30 seconds on medium power. Use room temperature cream cheese that is in block from, not a tub, for the best results.
    • For the pickle juice, simply use the pickle juice from the dill pickle jar.
    • Feel free to make this dip in advance and pull it out about 30 minutes prior to eating. The cream cheese will need to soften again in order for it to be a dippable consistency.
    • For a smoother dip, you can use an immersion blender or food processor to blend the ingredients together.
    • Add in some chopped green onions for a bit of color and flavor.
    • For a spicier dip, add in some diced jalapeños or hot sauce.
    • Consider adding other herbs and spices like parsley, chives, or onion powder.
    • For a cheesier dill pickle dip, add in half a cup of shredded cheddar cheese.

    Dill Pickle Dip

    This easy Dill Pickle Dip is creamy and smooth with amazing pickle flavor – the perfect appetizer for your next party! Serve this easy pickle dip recipe with your choice of dippers for a delicious appetizer everyone will love. Perfect for parties, game day and more!

    Course Appetizer Cuisine American Keyword dill pickle dip, dill pickle dip recipe, pickle dip, pickle dip recipe Prep Time 15 minutes minutes Servings 8 Calories 159kcal Author Trish – Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • 12 ounces cream cheese softened (full fat)
    • 2 tablespoons sour cream optional for a creamier dip
    • 4 tablespoons pickle juice
    • ¼ cup diced dill pickle
    • 1 to 2 teaspoons sweet relish
    • 1 teaspoon dried dill
    • 1 clove garlic minced
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • ¼ teaspoon black pepper

    Instructions

    • In a medium bowl, beat the cream cheese until you have a smooth and creamy texture. Mix in the sour cream here if using.m

    • Add in the pickle juice, diced dill pickle, sweet relish, dried dill, garlic, salt and pepper. Mix until everything is well combined.

    • Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve. Sit out at room temperature to soften if needed. Serve with your favorite crackers, chips or veggies!

    Notes

    Storage Information Store any leftover dip in an airtight container in the fridge. It will last for about 2 to 3 days. I don’t recommend freezing this dip because of the dairy.
