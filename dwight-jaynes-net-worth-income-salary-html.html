Dwight Jaynes works as a journalist and sportswriter in the city of Portland, in the United States. For many years, he worked as a sports writer for The Oregonian newspaper in Portland, Oregon. In 2001, he departed to write a weekly column for the nascent Portland Tribune and host a daily radio programme for KPAM, a talk radio station owned by the same corporation as the Tribune, which he did until he was fired in 200He has subsequently left his radio position to work full-time for the Tribune, where he served as the paper’s editor and sports writer until he was forced to resign at the end of July 2008 due to health reasons. Jaynes has been very critical of the administration of the NBA’s Portland Trail Blazers team, which is owned by Paul Allen. He has expressed specific dissatisfaction with the appointment and performance of Maurice Cheeks as head coach, among other things.

 He is also a vocal supporter of the expansion of Major League Baseball to Portland, as well as a vocal opponent of the University of Oregon. In addition to “The Long Hot Winter,” which he wrote with former Blazer coach Rick Adelman, he also co-authored “Against the World,” which he wrote with fellow Tribune writer Kerry Eggers. He has been awarded Oregon’s Sports Writer of the Year five times in his career. In 2011, Jaynes started working full-time for Comcast SportsNet Northwest, where he had co-hosted the Morning Sports Page on KXTG 95.5 The Game with Chad Doing and Antonio Harvey until 2011.
